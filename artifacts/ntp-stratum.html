Stratum 0
Reference Clocks
These are the ultimate authorities of time. Stratum 0 devices are high-precision timekeeping instruments such as Atomic Clocks, GPS satellites, or Radio Clocks. They do not sit on a network; instead, they are physically attached to a computer via a specialized interface. Because they provide the raw "true" time, they are the foundation upon which the entire system is built.

Stratum 1
Primary Time Servers
A computer directly connected to a Stratum 0 reference clock. These act as the primary gateways between the physical time source and the network. They are often referred to as Primary Time Servers. Their sole job is to act as a highly accurate buffer, taking the pulse from the atomic clock and making it available to other computers over the internet.

Stratum 2
Secondary Time Servers
These servers synchronize their time by requesting it from Stratum 1 servers. While there is a microscopic delay (jitter) introduced by the network connection, Stratum 2 servers are still incredibly accurate. They serve as a distribution layer, sharing the load so that thousands of devices don't overwhelm the handful of Primary servers. Most public NTP pools used by organizations are located at this level.

Stratum 3+
End Devices
End users such as your laptop, smartphone, or smart thermostat usually sit at Stratum 3 or 4. By the time the signal reaches your device, it has traveled through a few hops, but the synchronization remains accurate within milliseconds. This is more than enough precision to ensure your emails are timestamped correctly and your calendar alerts go off right on time.
