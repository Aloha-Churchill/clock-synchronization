Solar Time vs Mean Solar Time

A sundial tracks the real Sun, but the real Sun is an unreliable timekeeper. Earth's elliptical orbit and axial tilt cause solar noon to drift by up to ±16 minutes throughout the year. To get a steady clock, we invented a fiction: the mean sun, an imaginary body that travels at a constant speed along the celestial equator. Drag the slider to see the difference.
